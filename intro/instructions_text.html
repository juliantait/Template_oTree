Welcome. In this study you will play a simple two-person game called the Stag Hunt with another participant in this room. You will play {{ num_experimental_rounds }} rounds in total.

Your final payment will be the sum of your earnings from the rounds, plus a show-up fee of {{ showup }} EUR and a bonus of {{ quiz_bonus }} EUR if you answer every quiz question correctly on your first attempt.

The game

In each round you and your partner independently choose one of two actions: Stag or Hare. You make your choice without knowing what your partner picked.

Hunting a stag is only successful if both of you go for it. Hunting a hare always succeeds on your own, but the catch is smaller.

Your role

{% if treatment == "row" %}

In each cell of the matrix the two numbers are written as (partner's payoff, your payoff). Your earnings are always the second number in the cell.

What this means in practice

  • If both of you choose Stag, you each earn {{ stag_payoff }} points.
  • If both of you choose Hare, you each earn {{ hare_payoff }} points.
  • If you choose Stag but your partner chooses Hare, the Stag-chooser earns {{ stag_alone }} and the Hare-chooser earns {{ hare_payoff }}.

So Stag pays more if you coordinate, but Hare is a safer choice that pays the same regardless of what your partner does.
